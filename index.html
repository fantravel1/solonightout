The Cultural Shift

Solo dining is not awkward.
It's designed for you.

Counter seating, chef's tables, and bar dining were literally built for solo guests. The best restaurants in the world know this. Now you will too.

Counter Seating

Your Comfort, Your Control

Frequently Asked

Your questions, answered honestly

How do I go out alone without feeling awkward?

Going out alone is a skill you build, not an awkward situation you endure. Start with a low-pressure venue like a calm wine bar with counter seating or a late-night cafe with a good book. Choose places where solo guests are common — SoloNightOut rates every venue with a SoloScore™ so you know exactly how solo-friendly it is before you arrive.

Begin with our One Perfect Hour™ framework: one drink, one walk, one beautiful moment — then head home feeling accomplished, not exhausted. The more you do it, the more natural it becomes. Most people find that after the first 10 minutes, the awkwardness evaporates entirely.

What are the best cities for solo nights out?

Cities like Tokyo, Paris, Lisbon, Copenhagen, Melbourne, and Barcelona consistently rank highest on SoloNightOut's SoloScore™. These cities normalize solo dining, have excellent walkability, strong late-night cafe culture, and safe public transit.

Tokyo's izakaya counter culture, Paris's cafe-terrace tradition, and Lisbon's intimate wine bars make solo evenings feel natural and rewarding. Seoul's "honbap" (eating alone) culture means solo diners aren't just accepted — they're expected.

Is it normal to go out to dinner alone?

Completely normal — and increasingly celebrated. Solo dining is one of the fastest-growing trends in the restaurant industry. Counter seating, chef's tables, and bar dining were literally designed for solo guests.

In cities like Tokyo, Seoul, Paris, and New York, solo diners represent a significant portion of evening guests. The key is choosing venues with the right seating format — bar counters and chef's tables make solo dining feel intentional rather than conspicuous.

How do I sit at a bar alone confidently?

Sit at the bar counter (never a table for two), bring something to engage with — a book, a journal, your phone. Order with intention: ask the bartender for a recommendation. Own your space.

The secret: bartenders love solo guests. You're easier to serve and often more interesting to talk to. Choose venues during their calmer hours (early evening is ideal) and remember — nobody is watching you as much as you think they are.

What is SoloScore™ and how does it work?

SoloScore™ is SoloNightOut's proprietary rating system (1-100) that measures how comfortable a city or venue is for going out alone. It evaluates eight factors: Staff Respect, Seating Layout, Lighting, Noise Level, Safety Reality, Ease of Entry/Exit, Solo Normalization, and the "You Won't Feel Weird" Factor.

A score above 80 means you can walk in alone and feel completely natural. Every city and recommended venue on SoloNightOut carries a SoloScore so you can plan with confidence.

How do women go out alone safely?

Safety for women going out solo is about smart choices, not living in fear. Choose venues with respectful bar culture and attentive staff. Identify neighborhoods with active foot traffic and good lighting. Plan transport home before you go out. Share your location with a trusted contact.

SoloNightOut's women-forward guides highlight specific cafes, wine bars, and restaurants where women solo guests report feeling genuinely welcome and protected — city by city, neighborhood by neighborhood.

How do men go out alone without looking intrusive?

The key is calm, purposeful presence. Sit at the bar (not hovering near groups), engage with your own activity — reading, journaling, enjoying the food. Be responsive but not initiating with strangers. Respect personal space.

Choose venues where solo guests are common, which normalizes your presence immediately. SoloNightOut's men-forward guides offer city-specific advice on where solo men are completely normal and welcome.

What is a perfect one-hour solo night plan?

The One Perfect Hour™ is SoloNightOut's signature framework: arrive at a counter-seating restaurant at 7pm for one beautiful course, take a 20-minute walk through a well-lit neighborhood, stop at a wine bar for one glass at the counter, then head home.

The entire experience is roughly 60-90 minutes — enough to feel alive, not enough to feel drained. Every SoloNightOut city guide includes specific One Perfect Hour plans with exact venues, walking routes, and timing.

What are the safest neighborhoods for a solo evening?

Safe solo evening neighborhoods share key traits: well-lit streets, active foot traffic without chaos, proximity to reliable transit, a mix of restaurants and cafes (not just bars), and a culture where walking alone is completely normal.

SoloNightOut provides neighborhood-by-neighborhood comfort maps for every city we cover, with honest safety notes. Not to create fear — but to help you choose where you'll feel most at ease.

How do locals spend a solo evening in different cities?

Every SoloNightOut city guide includes a "How Locals Do Solo Nights" section. In Tokyo, locals frequent standing bars and ramen counters. In Paris, it's a cafe terrace with a book and a glass of wine. In Lisbon, locals walk the Alfama and stop for a pastel de nata and a ginjinha.

Understanding what's culturally normal in each city is the fastest way to feel comfortable going out alone there. When you do what locals do, you blend in naturally.
